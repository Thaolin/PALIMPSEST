# Vision

PALIMPSEST begins as a warm, legible survival RPG and gradually reveals that
its universe is written in an editable language.

> If it exists, the player can eventually open it.

The expressive north star is:

> Build it. Name the pattern. Teach reality what it does.

Stones, creatures, memories, diseases, civilizations, physical laws, gods, and
death can all possess inspectable source. Access deepens through five layers:

1. **State** — alter one particular apple.
2. **Behavior** — alter what that apple does when used.
3. **Lineage** — alter its descendants.
4. **Archetype** — redefine what an apple is.
5. **Law** — alter nourishment, identity, gravity, or death.

Normal play does not expose that source as a programming exercise. Knowledge
projects it through a structured **Lens**: typed Facets for State and readable
Clauses for Behavior and Law. Raw PALI is developer machinery and a possible
late root-level view. Access Depth determines which layer can be perceived,
Concept Access determines which meanings are legible or patchable, and Reach
determines whether a Patch binds to one Entity or something broader.

Early Reach stops at one Entity. A Patch affecting every apple is Prototype
Reach and belongs to late progression even though the engine supports and
tests that scope earlier. See [`INTERACTION.md`](INTERACTION.md) for the
interaction and progression contract.

The destination is ontology design rather than a catalogue of scripted jokes.
Edits should compose through ordinary system rules: a lie with mass falls under
gravity; a fire that burns names changes which named laws can match an entity.
Unexpected results should emerge from shared semantics.

## Semantic Construction

Survival, building, and combat are supporting layers. The defining player verb
is teaching the Universe new meaning through physical evidence:

1. **Observe** concepts as they manifest in different Entities.
2. **Arrange** material Entities into a bounded physical Arrangement.
3. **Name** the pattern by offering Exemplars and teaching an Archetype.
4. **Bind** known meaning or Behavior through a local Patch Anchor.
5. **Live with** every other thing that now satisfies the definition.

A ring of stones around a fire may become the first **Hearth**. The player can
eventually bind warmth to living things inside a Hearth. If the chosen
Archetype also admits a rib cage containing a candle, the resulting walking
Hearth is not a scripted joke: it is the Universe applying the player's own
definition correctly.

Construction is therefore physical syntax rather than a catalogue of recipes.
Spatial relations, materials, names, living components, boundaries, and learned
concepts can act as typed parts of a semantic machine. This is PALIMPSEST's
eventual counterpart to a wiring system: not signals travelling through blocks,
but meaning routed, filtered, inherited, inverted, and applied through
inspectable Clauses.

Early authored rules remain local because they require Patch Anchors. The
Patch Anchor makes experimentation material, destructible, and costly enough to
stay interesting. Later Knowledge may widen Reach, detach a rule from its
original construction, teach the Universe to recognize the construction
elsewhere, or admit it into a Law. The player becomes powerful by authoring a
local grammar and proving it through play, not by selecting `gravity = false`
from a cheat menu.

## Scarred Zones

Scarred Zones are the exploration counterpart to Semantic Construction: places
where another author has already changed how meaning resolves. A dungeon can be
a place where one sentence about reality has gone wrong.

Examples include:

- ripe things falling upward;
- fire consuming cold instead of fuel;
- spoken names acquiring mass and collecting as debris;
- dead creatures resolving as terrain;
- shadows becoming solid after sunset;
- damage becoming hunger inside a field of mercy.

These are not random glitch biomes. A Scarred Zone has a stable boundary,
traceable Provenance, and usually a Patch Anchor: a ruin, creature, inscription,
impossible tree, failed machine, or dead god. The changed rule remains
consistent inside its declared boundary. Destroying the sole active Patch Anchor
ends its anchored Patch unless the Patch was first stabilized, transferred, or
given another Patch Anchor. Understanding the Scar may let the player exploit,
transplant, repair, or worsen it.

Current Reach is population-based and has no spatial form. Scarred Zones must
therefore receive an explicit bounded-binding and conflict-precedence design
before implementation; they must not be disguised as Universe Reach or as an
Anomaly. An Anomaly is source that safely fails to compile or execute, while a
Scarred Zone is bizarre source working exactly as inscribed.

Knowledge determines how the same place is experienced. A novice sees floating
apples. A learned visitor perceives that mass resolves through something local.
An ascended visitor can inspect the altered Clause, identify its author and
Reach, and decide whether to submit a competing Patch. Layered zones become
archaeology: one civilization changed death, another attempted a repair, and
both definitions still leave readable Provenance.

The resulting exploration loop is:

> Enter a broken rule, survive its consequences, understand its grammar, and
> carry part of that grammar home.

A mature settlement can become a deliberate ecology of controlled Scars: a
greenhouse where winter is unrecognized, an orchard whose fruit harvests
itself, walls that make hostile intent heavy, or a tower whose interior and
exterior no longer agree.

## Survival and combat

Survival creates reasons to learn. Cold is first solved with fuel and shelter;
only later can a structure be taught to remember summer. Hunger, warmth,
weather, injury, and scarcity should ground the early game so semantic power
answers real material problems rather than existing as an abstract editor.

Combat tests the same grammar rather than becoming a separate ladder of weapon
numbers. A creature may be fought physically, trapped by a Semantic
Construction, excluded from an Archetype, or understood through a protected
dependency: a Glass Stag that cannot be wounded while reflected, a corpse that
fights because death no longer implies inactivity, or a god that protects the
definition of fire. Bosses can be arguments with reality.

## Coherent fever dream

PALIMPSEST must remain strange without becoming arbitrary:

1. Begin from an ordinary, legible baseline: apples fall, stones are heavy,
   fire warms, and shelter matters.
2. Let early Scarred Zones violate one understandable assumption at a time.
3. Telegraph symptoms before exposing vocabulary or source.
4. Make a changed rule consistent throughout its declared boundary and eligible
   population.
5. Preserve Provenance so consequences can be investigated rather than guessed.
6. Prefer semantic extrapolation over random corruption. If walls become
   edible, moths eating houses is a consequence; unrelated visual noise is not.
7. Make broader Reach produce broader responsibility, resistance, conflicts,
   or attention from authors invested in the previous definition.

Systemic roguelikes such as Caves of Qud are useful tonal and simulation
references, but the distinction is structural: those worlds contain strange
rules, while PALIMPSEST makes the authorship, scope, history, and eventual
editing of rules the play surface.

## Shareable histories

The compact save model makes a social layer possible without requiring a giant
content archive. A Universe can remain a deterministic seed plus sparse Scars,
Knowledge, and history. A future Scar Package can carry a compatible ordered
Patch and Scar history with its Provenance into another seed, letting players
fork, study, or deliberately invite fragments of somebody else's reality.
Cross-Genesis import must target portable semantic identities such as
Archetypes or versioned Site Blueprint kinds and roles, then apply an explicit
destination-selection rule for materialized Sites and Patch Anchors.
Seed-derived Entity and Site IDs cannot be replayed blindly, and arbitrary
native code is never a package dependency.

This need not begin as synchronous multiplayer. Finding another player's
haunted commit as a deterministic Scarred Zone already creates authorship,
mystery, and shareable stories while preserving the small Base C executable.

Large spatial features such as caves, villages, lakes, and ruins should come
from deterministic **Site Blueprints**, not frozen map stamps. A Site Blueprint
defines constraints, anchors, variation streams, and provenance so generated
places remain reproducible, composable, and eventually inspectable.

Player identity has three independent layers:

- **Universe**: physical state, history, entities, prototypes, and laws.
- **Knowledge**: understood language concepts, true names, and access rights.
- **Embodiment**: the creature presently inhabited and its physical needs.

The implemented 0.3 milestone keeps those stores separate and exposes the first
Knowledge-gated Lens: one Entity's nourishment is Patchable, Behavior is
readable, and mass progresses from Veiled Observation to qualitative meaning
and exact Notation without changing Universe or Embodiment. Raw source is
explicit developer machinery. The game remains a finite clearing with one
embodiment and no construction, Scarred Zones, or historical branching. The
visual language is an illuminated manuscript crossed with an old computer:
restrained ink and parchment, low-resolution silhouettes, firelight, and
syntax-like decorative marks whose meaning can later become literal.

The eventual save philosophy is:

> Save the recipe, the scars, and the things that acquired meaning.

Deterministic genesis supplies the recipe. Sparse materialized changes are the
scars. Later milestones may add append-only history, content-addressed law
patches, and ascended branches; this slice does not pretend those systems exist.
