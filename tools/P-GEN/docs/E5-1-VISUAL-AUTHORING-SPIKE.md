# E5.1 Visual-Authoring Spike

Status: **accepted in Palimpsest on 2026-07-21.**

The first E5 canonical bundle passed every automated reader, determinism,
packaging, and integration check, but failed player visual UAT. Connected
material families looked like a wall lattice and several important subjects
occupied too little of the native `20 × 20` cell.

The governing contract is:

`C:\DEV\PALIMPSEST\docs\P-GEN-E5-1-VISUAL-AUTHORING-SPIKE.md`

## P-GEN scope

- Give water, clouds, groves, ridges, crossings, and transitions distinct
  material grammar. Only actual connected structures retain generic cardinal
  connectors.
- Increase the authored occupancy of the current actor and landmark masks.
- Add representative visual-only surface-biome specimens where useful for
  review; do not invent Chronicle semantics.
- Keep `Chronicle.VisualPreview.Godot` pack-only.
- Add a separate `Chronicle.VisualWorkbench.Godot` authoring Adapter. It may
  reference the compiler, recompile catalogues in memory, inspect definitions,
  compare adjacency and display aspect, render a biome board, and explicitly
  save a bounded biome brief.
- Retain deterministic canonical output and the strict Palimpsest runtime seam.

The player supplied Caves of Qud's `16 × 24` sprite cell and `80 × 25` zone as
a comparison baseline. This spike retains native `20 × 20` pack data and fixes
occupancy and visual language first. A tall-cell workbench preview is evidence,
not a runtime contract change.

Reference-only concept evidence:
[`reference/e5-1-sprite-language-concept.png`](reference/e5-1-sprite-language-concept.png).
The shipped output remains indexed deterministic compiler data, not generated
bitmap art.

## Stop

Stop after complete P-GEN and Palimpsest automated proof plus a player launch
of the corrected world and workbench. Do not begin Goal 6A visual vocabulary.
